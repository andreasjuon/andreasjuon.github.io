# Power-Sharing and Democratic Stability

This article examines the relationship between power-sharing institutions and democratic stability using panel data analysis.

## Abstract

Power-sharing institutions are often proposed as solutions to ethnic conflict, but their effects on democratic stability remain contested.

## Methodology

We employ fixed-effects regression models to examine the relationship between power-sharing arrangements and democratic outcomes across multiple countries and time periods.

## Findings

Our analysis reveals that certain types of power-sharing arrangements are associated with improved democratic stability, while others may have negative effects.
